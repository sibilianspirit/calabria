Calabria, a land full of sunshine and history, conceals many secrets and wonders. One such place is MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti – a unique open-air art gallery located in the heart of Cosenza. This unconventional museum, accessible around the clock without tickets or restrictions, harmoniously integrates into the urban fabric, allowing everyone free contact with works of contemporary sculpture. Walking along Corso Mazzini, every visitor has the chance to discover an impressive collection of sculptures that enliven the urban space and give it a unique character.

Cosenza as an Open Gallery – History of MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti

MAB (Museo all'Aperto Bilotti) in Cosenza is an example of a civic art exhibition that goes beyond the traditional walls of a gallery. The project was born alongside the pedestrianisation and redesign of Corso Mazzini at the beginning of the 21st century, when the main promenade of the modern part of the city began its transformation into a representative civic axis. From the start, the museum was conceived as an open space, freely accessible to residents and visitors alike.

The idea for this exceptional space originated from the vision of Carlo Bilotti – an art collector and entrepreneur from Cosenza, connected to the American cosmetics industry. It was his name that gave the project its identity, and Corso Mazzini became the exhibition axis running from Piazza dei Bruzi to Piazza Carlo F. Bilotti. The exact date of inauguration is best verified through city documents or the official MAB website, as various simplified dates circulate.

After Carlo Bilotti's death in 2006, his family and local institutions continued to look after the legacy. Thanks to this, MAB has remained an important element of Cosenza's cultural life and one of the most recognisable symbols of the modern part of the city.

MAB changes the way art is perceived in an urban context, making Cosenza a place more open to everyday contact with culture. This is not a museum you have to make a special effort to enter – here sculptures are viewed between a café, a shop and an evening stroll. It is precisely this accessibility that is its greatest strength.

Art as an Element of Cosenza's Urban Transformation

Cosenza, situated in the heart of Calabria, has become a remarkable example of the connection between art and urban space. Its approach to urban planning integrates a prestigious collection with the daily rhythm of life, where sculptures stand alongside cafés and boutiques. It is on Corso Mazzini, the city's main street, that the renowned MAB transforms this space into a living gallery. Public art plays the role of a landmark here, influencing the way residents and visitors experience the modern city centre.

The history and heritage of Cosenza, full of ancient walls and palaces, give the city a distinctive character. The city is sometimes called the “Athens of Calabria” on account of its intellectual traditions, associated among others with the figure of Bernardino Telesio. MAB fits well into this narrative, connecting the contemporary axis of Corso Mazzini with the older, more historical face of Cosenza situated higher above the Crati valley.

Contemporary artistic projects, such as murals and installations, make Cosenza a centre for modern street art. Neighbourhoods that were once neglected now gain in attractiveness thanks to initiatives such as PAC – Piazza Arte Cosenza and the BoCs Art artist residencies. By assembling over 300 works by young creators, the city builds a multi-layered cultural narrative that engages the local community in the process of socio-economic revitalisation.

Such initiatives strengthen Cosenza's role as a city that treats culture not as decoration but as an element of everyday life. The increase in foot traffic and tourism around Corso Mazzini also supports gastronomy and commerce. Cosenza demonstrates that the “museum without walls” model can work not only symbolically but also very practically.

MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti on Corso Mazzini is a space where contemporary art enters into dialogue with the urban landscape. The open-air character of the collection means each work looks slightly different in the morning, in the harsh southern sun, and in the evening when the promenade fills with strolling residents. The Most Important Works in the Collection

The diversity of styles present in MAB is best discovered during a relaxed stroll, rather than by ticking off names from random lists. In practice, the greatest impression is made by the contrast itself between the movement of the city and the sculptures placed along Corso Mazzini. This is a collection best read as a whole – as a story about how art can transform an ordinary promenade into a place with character.

The Subtle Female Figure and Classical Beauty

In this part of the walk, the most interesting thing is observing how classicising and more figurative forms work in the open space of the city. Smooth silhouettes, calm proportions and a clear rendering of the human figure contrast well with the bustle of shops, windows and cafés. This is what makes MAB not just a collection of objects but also an interplay between sculpture and the everyday rhythm of the street.

Dynamic Wandering and Existential Unease

Some works on Corso Mazzini create an entirely different tension – more austere, vertical and focused on the human silhouette. In the open space of the promenade, such forms work particularly powerfully, as they are viewed in motion, from different perspectives and against the backdrop of urban bustle. It is at such moments that one best sees how MAB transforms an ordinary walk into a more attentive experience.

The Poetics of Obliteration and Futurist Energy

The open-air collection also showcases works that play with abbreviation, emptiness and bold contour. On the wide promenade, such forms do not get lost but rather catch the eye from afar and work well with light. This is what keeps MAB alive not just as a collection of sculptures but also as part of the urban scenography.

MAB in Cosenza – the Social Contribution of Street Art

MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti is not just a gallery but an essential element of the social landscape. Thanks to complete accessibility 24 hours a day, art has ceased to be the domain of elites, becoming part of everyday life for all social groups. This is a real example of the democratisation of culture, where economic barriers practically disappear.

Culture is a pillar of Cosenza's identity, and MAB influences the way residents engage with the city. Daily contact with art in public space naturally familiarises people with sculpture and encourages more attentive observation of their surroundings. The museum also fulfils an educational function, as even a short walk along Corso Mazzini can serve as a first encounter with contemporary art.

An important aspect is also the very presence of works in a living urban environment. The sculptures are exposed to light, weather and intensive foot traffic, which means their reception is always linked with responsibility for shared space. This is one of the reasons why MAB functions not just as a tourist attraction but also as a daily lesson in sharing the city.

MAB serves as an inspiration for urban planners and those involved in urban culture, demonstrating that integrating art with everyday life truly works. The collaboration between the Bilotti family and public administration has created a model of patronage that has clearly influenced Cosenza's image. Thanks to this, residents and visitors can experience art not on special occasions but simply during a stroll.

Practical Information

Location: Corso Mazzini, Cosenza – from Piazza dei Bruzi to Piazza Carlo F. Bilotti
Hours: Accessible 24/7
Tickets: Free admission
Getting there: Cosenza railway station is outside the pedestrian area; Corso Mazzini can be reached on foot from the modern city centre or by city bus to the area around Piazza dei Bruzi or Piazza Bilotti.

Walking through the entire MAB axis usually takes about 20–30 minutes without stops and 45–60 minutes when viewing the sculptures at a relaxed pace. The easiest approach is to start at Piazza dei Bruzi and walk towards Piazza Carlo F. Bilotti, treating the route as a simple, linear stroll through the centre.

From Lamezia Terme Airport to Cosenza is about 65 km. By car the journey usually takes 45–60 minutes; for bus or train connections, it is best to check current timetables before travelling.

The most pleasant time to visit MAB is late afternoon or evening, especially from spring to early autumn, when Corso Mazzini truly comes alive. In summer, daytime temperatures in Cosenza often exceed 30°C, so it is worth having water, a hat and sun protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is MAB and what sculptures can you see on the streets of Cosenza?

MAB is the Museo all'Aperto Bilotti, an open-air contemporary sculpture gallery stretched along Corso Mazzini. The collection is best explored on site and through official MAB materials, as online lists of works can be imprecise or outdated.

Where exactly is the MAB Bilotti open-air gallery located?

The museum stretches along Cosenza's main promenade – Corso Mazzini. The collection starts at Piazza dei Bruzi and leads to Piazza Carlo Bilotti, creating an artistic axis in the modern city centre.

Who was Carlo Bilotti and why does the museum bear his name?

Carlo Bilotti was an art collector and entrepreneur from Cosenza, connected to the American cosmetics industry. It was his vision of a civic art exhibition that strongly influenced the character of MAB, and the museum therefore bears his name.

Is visiting MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti free?

Yes, visiting the museum is completely free. The sculptures are placed in public space, allowing them to be admired at any time of day or night without the need to purchase tickets.

What new works were added to the collection in 2026?

Verified information about recent additions to the collection is best checked through official MAB channels or municipal communications in Cosenza, as details circulated online may not be up to date.
