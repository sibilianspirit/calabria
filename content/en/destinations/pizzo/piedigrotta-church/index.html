Piedigrotta Church is a cave chapel carved into tufa rock directly above the sea – one of the most extraordinary sacred sites in all of Italy. Created out of gratitude by shipwrecked sailors, then expanded over decades by local sculptors who coaxed dozens of biblical figures from the living rock. It is a place where nature, faith, and folk art intertwine in a way you will not find anywhere else in Pizzo or the whole of Calabria.

The shipwreck legend – how the church was born

In the late 17th century, a Neapolitan merchant vessel sailed into a violent storm off the coast of Pizzo. As waves smashed the hull, the captain and crew prayed before the ship’s painting of the Madonna di Piedigrotta, vowing to build a chapel in her honour if they survived. The ship sank, but every sailor reached the shore alive.

On the beach, among the wreckage, they found the painting of the Madonna and the ship’s bell intact. They took it as a divine sign – placed the painting in a small grotto hollowed into the tufa cliff, and so the Chiesetta di Piedigrotta was born. That painting remains the chapel’s focal point and an object of popular devotion to this day.

Sculptures from rock – the work of the Barone family

For nearly two centuries, the chapel remained a modest place of prayer. Everything changed around 1880, when Angelo Barone, a local shopkeeper with an artistic soul, decided to expand the grotto and fill it with sculptures. Working by hand in the soft tufa, he carved saints, scenes from Scripture, and episodes from the life of Christ.

After Angelo’s death, his son Alfonso Barone continued the work, adding more figures and deepening the cave interior. Together, father and son created an underground sculpture gallery in which dozens of life-size figures emerge directly from the walls and ceiling. The effect is extraordinary – in the pale light filtering through the entrance, the figures seem to come alive.

Among the most notable scenes:

  • The Nativity – Mary, Joseph, and shepherds carved into a natural rock niche
  • The Last Supper – Jesus with twelve apostles at a table, each face individually rendered
  • The Crucifixion – a monumental scene deep within the grotto, lit by natural light from above
  • Madonna di Piedigrotta – the original 17th-century painting from the shipwreck, set in the central altar niche
  • Saint George and the Dragon – patron saint of Pizzo, carved in one of the side niches

Tufo (tufa), the rock from which the cliff is made, is a soft, porous limestone – easy to carve when wet but hardening as it dries. This property allowed the Barones to work without specialised tools, using mainly a chisel and hammer.

Visiting and practical information

The chapel sits at the base of the cliff along Via Riviera Prangi, in Località Madonnella, a few hundred metres south of Pizzo’s centre. Access is via steep steps leading downward – comfortable shoes are recommended. Admission costs a few euros, and the interior stays cool even on hot days, so a light layer is useful.

The church is open to visitors from spring through autumn, with the longest hours in July and August. Come in the morning before the crowds arrive – the atmosphere in the silent grotto is at its most powerful then. Photography is allowed (no flash).

A special day is 2 July – the Feast of the Madonna di Piedigrotta. A distinctive sea procession takes place: the painting is carried from the grotto and transported by a boat called the Perla Nera, accompanied by a flotilla of decorated fishing boats. It is preceded by a nine-day novena. This is one of the most evocative religious celebrations on the Calabrian coast.

After your visit, combine it with Castello Murat in the town centre and a stroll ending at Piazza della Repubblica, where a tartufo di Pizzo awaits.

Piedigrotta Church – frequently asked questions

Who carved the statues in Piedigrotta Church?

The sculptures are the work of Angelo Barone, a local shopkeeper who began around 1880, and his son Alfonso, who continued after his father’s death. Both carved the figures by hand in the soft tufa (porous limestone) that forms the cliff.

How was the cave church created?

In the late 17th century, Neapolitan sailors survived a shipwreck near Pizzo. On the beach they found a painting of the Madonna that had survived the wreck and placed it in a grotto in the cliff, fulfilling a vow made during the storm. The simple chapel was later expanded by the Barone family in the 19th century.

Is Piedigrotta Church open all year?

The chapel is open mainly from spring to autumn, with the longest hours in July and August. Winter hours may be limited. Admission costs a few euros.

When does the sea procession of the Madonna di Piedigrotta take place?

The feast day falls on 2 July. The painting is carried from the grotto and transported by the boat Perla Nera, accompanied by a flotilla of decorated fishing boats. It is preceded by a nine-day novena.
