Calabria, a land full of sunshine and history, conceals many secrets and wonders. One such place is MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti – a unique open-air art gallery located in the heart of Cosenza. This unconventional museum, accessible around the clock without tickets or restrictions, harmoniously integrates into the urban fabric, allowing everyone free contact with works by the most renowned artists of the 20th century. Walking along the historic Corso Mazzini, every visitor has the chance to discover an impressive collection of sculptures that enliven the urban space and give it a unique character.

Cosenza as an Open Gallery – History of MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti

MAB (Museo all'Aperto Bilotti) in Cosenza is an example of an innovative approach to presenting art, far beyond the traditional walls of a gallery. The project was officially inaugurated in March 2005 under the aegis of UNESCO, although the first steps were taken as early as 2002 with the pedestrianisation of the city's main artery. This unique museum currently displays 40 twentieth-century sculptures, creating a democratic space accessible to every resident and tourist free of charge.

The idea for this exceptional space originated from the vision of Carlo Bilotti – an entrepreneur from Cosenza who found success in New York in the cosmetics industry. Bilotti, a friend of such luminaries as Andy Warhol and Giorgio de Chirico, wished to perform an act of cultural restitution towards his hometown. The city's main avenue, Corso Mazzini, became the axis of the exhibition stretching over 1.2 kilometres, from Piazza dei Bruzi to the square named after the founder.

After Carlo's death in November 2006, his brother Enzo continued the mission, followed by Roberto Bilotti Ruggi d'Aragona. Thanks to their commitment, the museum gained official recognition from the Italian Ministry of Culture in 2019 as a collection of exceptional historical significance. A breakthrough moment was the expansion in March 2026, when, thanks to funding exceeding 64,000 euros, 10 new works were installed, including pieces by Futurists and classics of Italian sculpture.

MAB interactively changes the way art is perceived in an urban context, making Cosenza a centre for artistic space open to dialogue and education. These initiatives, supported by local authorities under Mayor Franz Caruso, contribute to enhancing the city's reputation. Contemporary Cosenza aspires to inscription on the UNESCO World Heritage List, combining the Bilotti legacy with modern technologies such as QR codes and stage lighting systems.

Art as an Element of Cosenza's Urban Transformation

Cosenza, situated in the heart of Calabria, has become a remarkable example of symbiosis between art and urban space. Its unique approach to urban planning integrates a prestigious collection with the daily rhythm of life, where sculptures stand alongside cafes and boutiques. It is on Corso Mazzini, the city's main street, that the renowned MAB transforms this space into a living gallery. Public art plays a psychogeographic role here, influencing residents' emotions and becoming a landmark in the modern city centre.

The history and heritage of Cosenza, full of ancient walls and Renaissance palaces, give the city a unique character. Street sculptures not only embellish the space but also enrich local identity, connecting past with present. Thanks to monuments such as the one commemorating the philosopher Bernardino Telesio, the city gains in historical significance. MAB serves as a bridge between this tradition and modernity, introducing surrealist and metaphysical forms into the historic urban context.

Contemporary artistic projects, such as murals and installations, make Cosenza a centre for modern street art. Neighbourhoods that were once neglected now gain in attractiveness thanks to initiatives such as PAC – Piazza Arte Cosenza and the BoCs Art artist residencies. By assembling over 300 works by young creators, the city builds a multi-layered cultural narrative that engages the local community in the process of socio-economic revitalisation.

Initiatives such as the Crack Fumetti e Dintorni festival help combat exclusion by changing perceptions of public spaces on Corso Telesio and Lungo Crati. The increase in tourist traffic generated by MAB directly stimulates the local economy, supporting gastronomy and commerce. Cosenza proves that the “museum without walls” model is an effective response to the crisis of traditional cultural institutions, attracting visitors from around the world.

Masterpieces Open on the Street – Key Works of MAB in Cosenza

MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti on Corso Mazzini is a space where contemporary art enters into dialogue with the landscape. The diversity of materials – from bronze and travertine to marmo nero marquinia – means the collection constantly changes its appearance depending on the light and weather conditions. Each of the 40 sculptures is an accessible lesson in 20th-century art history.

The Most Important Works in the Collection

The diversity of styles present in MAB is impressive and encompasses the most important movements of the past century. A special place is occupied by the work of Giorgio de Chirico, whose sculptures such as I due archeologi and Il Grande Metafisico bring an atmosphere of suspended time and intellectual reflection into the heart of the city.

The Subtle Female Figure and Classical Beauty

Emilio Greco enriched the collection with works such as La Grande Bagnante n.2 and L'Accoccolata. These sculptures celebrate femininity through flowing lines and soft volumes, providing an aesthetic contrast to the austere urban architecture. Meanwhile, the works of Mario Sironi, such as the marble Donna acefala seduta, exude the monumental power characteristic of the interwar period.

Dynamic Wandering and Existential Unease

The elongated figure by Alberto Giacometti illustrates human striving in an uncertain reality. The collection also features notable works by Giacomo Manzù, including the famous Grande Cardinale in piedi. This hieratic figure, enclosed in an almost abstract cone, symbolises spiritual solitude and authority, being one of the most photographed points on Corso Mazzini.

The Poetics of Obliteration and Futurist Energy

Sasha Sosno, a French artist, introduced the technique of obliteration to Cosenza in works such as Bronzi di Riace and Sette di cuori. The artist plays with emptiness, encouraging the viewer to mentally complete the missing forms. New energy was brought to the museum by Futurist installations, including Giacomo Balla's Linee forza del pugno, which with its vivid red colour and geometric form catches the eye of passers-by.

MAB in Cosenza – the Social Contribution of Street Art

MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti is not just a gallery but an essential element of the social landscape. Thanks to complete accessibility 24 hours a day, art has ceased to be the domain of elites, becoming part of everyday life for all social groups. This is a real example of the democratisation of culture, where economic and class barriers are completely removed.

Culture is a pillar of Cosenza's identity, and MAB actively influences residents' sensibility. Daily exposure to works by Salvador Dalí and Umberto Mastroianni naturally educates and shapes aesthetic taste. The museum fulfils an educational function through workshops and multimedia systems, particularly engaging young people in dialogue about the role of art in society.

An important aspect is also the protection and conservation of these works in a challenging urban environment. In 2024, following damage to the Stele di Sosno, the city implemented a rigorous protection plan under the guidance of specialists from Bretia Restauri. The application of nanotechnology and modern monitoring allows the patina and structure of the sculptures to be preserved for future generations, strengthening civic responsibility for the common good.

MAB serves as an inspiration for urban planners worldwide, proving that integrating art with urban life stimulates development. The collaboration between the Bilotti family and public administration has created a unique model of patronage that has transformed Cosenza into a thriving cultural centre. Thanks to this, residents and visitors can experience beauty as an integral part of their living environment, significantly improving quality of life in the city.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is MAB and what sculptures can you see on the streets of Cosenza?

MAB is the Museo all'Aperto Bilotti, an open-air contemporary sculpture gallery. You can admire 40 works by such masters as Giorgio de Chirico, Salvador Dalí, Giacomo Balla, and Amedeo Modigliani, made from bronze, marble, and iron.

Where exactly is the MAB Bilotti open-air gallery located?

The museum stretches along Cosenza's main promenade – Corso Mazzini. The collection starts at Piazza dei Bruzi and leads to Piazza Carlo Bilotti, creating an artistic axis in the modern city centre.

Who was Carlo Bilotti and why does the museum bear his name?

Carlo Bilotti was an entrepreneur and art collector from Cosenza who was active in the USA. He donated his private collection of masterpieces to the city, wanting to make art accessible to all residents of his hometown.

Is visiting MAB Museo all'Aperto Bilotti free?

Yes, visiting the museum is completely free. The sculptures are placed in public space, allowing them to be admired at any time of day or night without the need to purchase tickets.

What new works were added to the collection in 2026?

In March 2026, the museum was enriched with 10 new works, including Giacomo Balla's Futurist “Linee forza del pugno,” sculptures by Gino Severini, and monumental marbles by Mario Sironi.
